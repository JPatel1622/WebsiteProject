What is a wildfire?

A wildfire is is a fire of vegetation that is uncontrolled and unplanned. Wildfires are known to devastate large areas and make the land more prone to flooding. Falling embers expand the wildfire and the smoke caused by the fire causes health issues for nearby communities.

How to prepare for a wildfire?

Always have an evacuation plan ready for an unexpected fire. Close windows and any other openings to keep the smoke out of the house. Secure food and water, and wear protective gear to prevent carbon monoxide poisoning. After the fire, stay alert for broken traffic lights and street signs, be on the lookout for debris on the road and drive during daylight to avoid hazards.
